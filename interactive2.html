A challenge is an attempt to remove or restrict materials, based upon the objections of a person or group.

More than 11,000 books have been challenged since 1982.

The main reason that books are being challenged and banned is that people are concerned for the influence they have on their children.

The problem here is that most of these books shine a light on issues we are and should be talking about as a society. Youth fiction is a main target.

Orwell's 1984 is a heavily challenged book. The ALA routinely lists it as one of the most challenged books and it's in the top 10 list of challenged classics. The book warns of totalitarian censorship and chronicles the grim future of a society robbed of free will, privacy and truth.

Meyer's Twilight series was ranked No. 5 on the ALA's report of challenged books in 2010. The stories are a supernatural saga of vampires and teen romance. They were removed from numerous school libraries for having content that was too sexual and going against religious beliefs.
